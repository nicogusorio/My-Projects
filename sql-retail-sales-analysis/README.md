# SQL Retail Sales Analysis

This project analyzes retail sales data using SQL to identify key business insights.

## Objectives
- Identify top customers
- Analyze sales by product category
- Evaluate regional sales performance
- Assess category profitability

## SQL Techniques Used
- JOIN
- GROUP BY
- Aggregation (SUM)
- ORDER BY
- LIMIT
- Table creation

## Dataset
Sample Superstore dataset.

## Tools
- SQL
- SQLite
- DB Browser for SQLite

## Key Findings

### Top Customers
The analysis shows that a small group of customers generates significantly higher sales than the rest of the customer base. Ken Lonsdale recorded the highest total sales ($141K), followed by Sanjit Engle ($134K) and Adrian Barton ($130K).

### Sales by Category
Technology generated the highest total sales ($893K), followed by Furniture ($764K) and Office Supplies ($736K).

### Profitability by Category
Technology produced the highest profit ($153K), followed by Office Supplies ($126K). Furniture generated significantly lower profit ($20K) despite relatively high sales.

### Regional Performance
The West region generated the highest total sales ($4.9M), followed by the East ($4.16M) and Central ($3.75M). The South region recorded the lowest sales ($2.7M).

## Conclusion

The analysis highlights several important patterns within the retail sales dataset. 
Technology products generate the highest revenue and profit, indicating strong demand and profitability within this category. 
Although Furniture produces high sales, its profit margins are significantly lower, suggesting potential cost or pricing challenges.

Customer analysis shows that a relatively small group of customers contributes a large share of total revenue, emphasizing the importance of maintaining relationships with high-value customers.

Regional performance analysis reveals that the West region drives the highest sales, while the South region has the lowest overall revenue, which may indicate potential opportunities for growth or targeted marketing strategies.
